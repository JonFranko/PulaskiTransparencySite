What This Is:

For too long, our township has mistaken more meetings for more transparency. Most people can't take off work, hire babysitters, or sit through hours of discussion just to stay informed.

So instead of showing up to their meetings, I'm bringing the meetings to you.

This project collects, transcribes, and summarizes official township meeting recordings to provide an accessible record of what's really happening in our local government.

How It Works:

Step 1: I request official meeting recordings through Pennsylvania's Right-to-Know process (as of creating this website the township has been uncooperative with proactively providing information in the name of transparency).

Step 2: Each recording is transcribed using an open-source Python-based system that runs locally (no cloud storage or data selling).

Step 3: The transcript is summarized using AI to cut through filler and produce a clear, factual overview of what was said — including:

  • Main discussion topics
  • Public concerns
  • Decisions and agreements
  • Notable moments

These are no-spin summaries. They may not be perfect (transcription depends on how clearly people speak) but they're a step in the right direction.

Why It Matters:

Transparency isn't about having more meetings. It's about making information public and accessible — without barriers.

When residents can review meetings on their own time, they can ask better questions, hold leaders accountable, and participate without being shut out by scheduling or politics.

This project is meant to reduce confusion, not create it, and to replace gossip and gatekeeping with data, documentation, and plain language.

The Bigger Picture:

In small-town politics, information is power. But power only balances when everyone has access to the same information.

By publishing these records, we move away from one person or faction controlling the story and toward a community that's informed, empowered, and involved.

Disclaimers:

  • Transcripts are machine-generated and may contain inaccuracies.
  • Summaries are automated analyses, not official statements or legal documents.
  • Audio files are provided as-is, with no editing or commentary.
  • If you believe something is misrepresented, contact me.

This isn't about picking sides. It's about reclaiming local government as a public good: something we can all see, understand, and improve together.

"Truth never damages a cause that is just."
