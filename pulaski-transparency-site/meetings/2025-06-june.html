June 2025 Supervisors Meeting

Date: June 2, 2025 Type: Regular Monthly Meeting Status: Transcribed & Summarized

Meeting Recording

Meeting Summary

Approval of Minutes and Bills

  • The board approved minutes from the May 5 meeting and authorized payments across several funds totaling roughly $66,500 (general, state, fire, and light funds)

Personnel and Hiring

  • The township voted to rehire Greg as an as-needed part-time employee at $18.50/hour
  • Discussion followed about hiring temporary help to mow cemeteries, ballfields, and parks, especially due to an employee's injury. Pay was set at $16/hour, with temporary arrangements made until her recovery (expected 8 weeks)
  • Two individuals were approved to share mowing duties temporarily without advertising the position publicly

Subdivision and Consolidation

  • The supervisors approved a three-lot consolidation for Steve and Molly Slick, combining about 141.72 acres into a single parcel

Police Department Agreements

  • The board reviewed and forwarded the police chief job description and personnel policy to the police association and Teamsters union for feedback
  • A Memorandum of Agreement (MOA) summarizing police contract terms was nearly finalized; minor issues (e.g., longevity pay, GPS vehicle policy) remained before signature

Department Reports

Fire Department:

  • 20 total calls (no structure fires); assisted with Memorial Day services

Road Department:

  • Road patching and cemetery maintenance completed; collaboration noted among community volunteers

Police Department:

  • Routine incidents—fraud, assault, traffic citations, and one missing person located

Park Committee Report

  • Discussed grant application for park upgrades, including ADA walkways, pavilion lighting, and signage
  • Total request: $24,673 (no township match required)
  • Next meeting scheduled for June 30, 2025

Resident Complaint – Tax Collection and Sharp Collections

  • A resident (Jonathan Franko) raised concerns about misapplied payments and unprofessional behavior from the township's former debt collector, Sharp Collections
  • He argued that a $19 payment intended for taxes was misdirected and requested reimbursement of $30.50
  • Tone: Frustrated but respectful

Board Response

  • The chair stated it was a matter between the resident and the collection agency, not the township
  • The resident disagreed and vowed to pursue the issue further

Employment Clarification

  • A resident asked about part-time hiring decisions; the board clarified Greg's status and the need for temporary help during road maintenance and tar and chip operations
  • Approved May meeting minutes and all fund payments
  • Rehired Greg as part-time employee at $18.50/hour
  • Authorized two temporary part-time hires for mowing duties at $16/hour
  • Approved Slick subdivision consolidation
  • Forwarded police job description and personnel policy to unions for review
  • Tabled police MOA pending minor revisions
  • Recognition given to volunteers who maintained cemeteries and parks
  • Township official noted collaboration with neighboring municipalities (Wilmington and Mahoning) for shared road maintenance
  • A resident announced certification of the township as a National Wildlife Federation habitat site
  • Next regular meeting scheduled for July 7, 2025, at 7 PM
