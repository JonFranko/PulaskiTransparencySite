February 2025 Supervisors Meeting

Date: February 2025 Type: Regular Monthly Meeting Status: Transcribed & Summarized

Meeting Recording

Listen to the complete, unedited meeting recording:

Meeting Summary

Meeting Minutes & Financial Approvals

  • Approved minutes from January 6 and January 24 meetings
  • Approved payment of bills:
    • General Fund: $25,001.28
    • State Fund: $11,164.12
    • Fire Fund: $8,206.63
    • Liquid Fuel: $304.69

Salt Contract

  • Approved ordering 600 tons of salt through CoStars for 2025-2026 season

Employee Wage Adjustment

  • Voted to raise an employee's hourly rate from $21.00 to $22.50

Elected Tax Collector Compensation

  • Adopted a resolution setting compensation at 5% of collected taxes through 2026

Equipment Sales and Repairs

  • Approved sale of Vermeer wood chipper to Austin Dunkle for $1,000
  • Discussed sale of a used grader to a buyer for $9,000 pending written agreement
  • Tabled decision on repairing or reselling a 2016 truck

Supervisor Compensation Ordinance

  • Discussed advertising intent to adopt Ordinance 1-2025, increasing supervisor pay to $3,145 annually ($262/month) as permitted for townships under 4,999 residents

Property Consolidation

  • Approved subdivision and consolidation of three township-owned lots (approx. 12 acres) into one parcel at an estimated cost of $3,130, to be paid from the general fund

Park Rentals

  • Approved park rental requests for January 26 and February 16

Fire Department

  • 14 total calls in January (including brush fires, traffic assists, and EMS calls)
  • Mentioned upcoming sale of old engine and progress on completing the new one

Road Department

  • Reported winter maintenance, salt use, and ongoing drainage issues on Copper Hill, Deer Creek, and Victoria Lane
  • Discussed liability concerns over a tree on private property and potential drainage repairs requiring excavation and French drains

Police Department

  • Reported 36 total calls including forced entry, theft, harassment, accidents, and drug-related activity

Community Committee

  • Working on DCNR grant applications, Arbor Day activities, and collaborations with Boy Scouts and local garden clubs

Resident Presentation – Farm Stand Zoning Exception

  • A resident requested permission to operate a small farm stand on a 10-acre property, citing the Pennsylvania Right to Farm Act and agricultural preservation goals
  • Requested reconsideration of current zoning that prohibits farm stands in residential/agriculture areas
  • Expressed frustration over restrictive ordinances and proposed allowing exceptions for properties ≥10 contiguous acres
  • Supervisors and solicitor expressed general support and discussed drafting an ordinance amendment to permit small roadside stands with safety considerations (e.g., parking, visibility)

Discussion Follow-Up

  • Agreement to draft language for review at the March 2025 meeting
  • Recommendation for the resident to gauge neighbor sentiment due to potential concerns about traffic or nuisance
  • Approved minutes and financials
  • Approved multiple motions (salt purchase, wage increase, tax collector pay, subdivision costs, park rentals)
  • Planned to advertise Ordinance 1-2025 on supervisor pay increases
  • Agreed to draft an ordinance amendment for farm stand allowances for review at the next meeting
  • Deferred decision on truck repair pending further information
  • Some light humor and informal conversation among supervisors
  • Public comment period included open discussion on zoning, property rights, and neighbor relations
  • Tone was cooperative with some confusion over historical zoning rules and property boundaries
