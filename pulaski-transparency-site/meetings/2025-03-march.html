March 2025 Supervisors Meeting

Date: March 3, 2025 Type: Regular Monthly Meeting Status: Transcribed & Summarized

Meeting Recording

Meeting Summary

Labor Relations Complaint

  • Discussion of a prior hiring dispute that required township legal defense
  • Solicitor explained role and necessity of representation

Villa Maria Conservancy Partnership

  • Representatives from the Sisters and the Foundation for Sustainable Forests presented a proposed partnership to protect 670 acres of forest and fields
  • Plan involves transferring ownership to the Foundation while keeping the land as a working forest under DCNR Community Conservation Partnerships Program grant funding
  • The township was asked to provide a letter of support for the grant application

Routine Approvals

  • Approved minutes from the February 3 meeting
  • Approved payment of bills:
    • General Fund: $18,342.42
    • State Fund: $18,804.63
    • Fire Fund: $4,381.36
    • Liquid Fuels: $467.65

Zoning Board Vacancy

  • Scott Phillips relocating; seat to be advertised for a new 3-year term

Supervisor Compensation Ordinance (No. 1-2025)

  • Adopted raising supervisor pay to $3,145/year ($262/month) per updated state allowance

Truck Buyback and Repair

  • Discussed repurchasing a 2016 Ford F-550 previously declared a total loss
  • Insurance settlement: township to receive $46,500 and repurchase for $12,000 (effective $9,000 after valuation adjustment)
  • Plan to repair and resell with a reserve price of $30,000 after obtaining an R title

Material Bids

  • Authorized advertising for bids for stone, asphalt, oil, and fuel supplies for 2025 road maintenance

Police Department

  • Promoted an officer to full-time at $26/hour
  • Discussed adopting a body-worn camera policy required for state grant compliance

Solar Energy Escrow Agreement

  • Solicitor reported ongoing review of decommissioning escrow terms for Villa Maria's solar project
  • Decision tabled pending attorney feedback

Subdivision Approvals

  • Approved Villa Maria campus subdivision (separating forest from developed areas)
  • Approved David P. Shields subdivision adjustment

Grant Resolutions

  • Adopted Resolution 5-2025, authorizing a DCNR grant application for park and playground improvements

Farm Stand Ordinance

  • Reviewed proposed Ordinance 2-2025 to allow farm stands in residential districts as conditional uses (5-acre minimum)
  • Debate over $800 conditional-use fee and whether this conflicts with the PA Right to Farm Act
  • Decision: motion to advertise was rescinded; issue tabled for further legal and policy review

Zoning Ordinance Overhaul

  • Discussion of rewriting the entire township zoning ordinance, estimated to take one year and cost $20,000â€“$30,000
  • Consideration of hiring a professional consultant to align zoning with community goals and infrastructure constraints

Material Storage Building

  • Explored grant and cost options for a new covered storage structure for salt and aggregate materials

Park Committee

  • Updates on DCNR grant coordination, walking trail planning, and tree-planting partnerships
  • Township committed $30,032.50 toward the playground grant match

Farm Stand Applicant

  • Expressed frustration over new $800 fee, claiming the township is violating state agricultural protections and overregulating small farms
  • Some residents voiced support for reducing bureaucracy; others emphasized road safety and fairness

Fire Department & Emergency Services

  • Concerns raised about lack of local ambulance coverage and restrictive state rules preventing cross-border response from nearby units
  • Discussion highlighted volunteer shortages and burnout within rural EMS services
  • Adopted Ordinance 1-2025 (supervisor compensation)
  • Approved truck buyback plan and sale preparation
  • Approved Resolution 5-2025 for DCNR playground grant
  • Approved zoning board seat advertisement
  • Tabled farm stand ordinance and solar escrow agreement
  • Authorized zoning rewrite exploration and potential consultant engagement
  • Approved purchase of new office copier ($2,395 + $300/year maintenance)
  • Approved street sweeping contract (3 days, $175/hour, paid from State Fund)
  • Township officials debated the value of newspaper legal ads versus online postings
  • Lengthy and emotional discussion about rural identity, zoning fairness, and government overreach
  • Supervisor announced intention to run for re-election and invited residents to sign petitions
