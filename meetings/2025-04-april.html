April 2025 Supervisors Meeting

Date: April 7, 2025 Type: Regular Monthly Meeting Status: Transcribed & Summarized

Meeting Recording

Meeting Summary

Liquid Fuels Payment

  • The township received $181,041.11 from the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania

Executive Sessions

  • Held March 3 and April 1 to discuss labor and personnel matters

Material Bids

  • Stone, asphalt, oil, and diesel bids reviewed; contracts awarded to various suppliers including Russell Standard, Lindy Paving, and Evergreen
  • Road projects: Time Chirping Road chosen for a liquid fuels funding application due by April 30

Zoning Hearing Board Appointment

  • Two applicants (Ruth Fletcher and Shane Santiago) presented
  • The board appointed Shane Santiago for a three-year term based on qualifications in management and community leadership

Solar Panel Escrow Agreement (Villa Maria Project)

  • Approved to ensure future decommissioning costs are covered through a cash escrow account updated every five years

Roadside Stand Ordinance Amendment

  • Discussion to allow stands in residential districts (minimum three acres, parking requirements, signage rules)
  • Public hearing scheduled for May 5, 2025, at 6:45 PM

Park Rules Ordinance

  • Draft finalized with addition of catch-and-release only fishing policy; to be advertised for adoption

Subdivision Approval

  • Shaler-Riley Subdivision approved, including sewage facilities planning and non-building declarations

Conservation Club Donations

  • Township contributed $240, bringing total donations to $2,000

Police Department Policies

  • Adoption of updated media and body camera policies following a state police audit

Body Camera Grant

  • Township approved purchase of 8 body cameras ($11,010.94 total, five-year lease), funded through an $8,000 grant and gas fund balance

Property Sale

  • Board rescinded a prior offer and accepted a new $9,000 offer from Horizon Construction Group for the old furnace property

Ball Field Improvements

  • Approved purchase of new bases, pitching mound, and sand ($2,940 total) for township parks

Police Staffing

  • Multiple residents and supervisors expressed frustration with insufficient police coverage and delays in emergency response
  • Calls for hiring more officers and reinstating a Police Chief position

Property Maintenance

  • Residents raised concerns about unregistered vehicles and junk accumulation
  • Township discussed citation limitations and potential use of property liens for cleanup enforcement

Road Safety

  • Request for a traffic mirror on New Bedford-Sharon Road due to pedestrian safety concerns
  • Approved materials bids and fuel purchases
  • Chose Time Chirping Road for 2025 liquid fuels grant
  • Appointed Shane Santiago to Zoning Hearing Board
  • Authorized solar escrow agreement for Villa Maria project
  • Approved advertisement and public hearing for roadside stand ordinance
  • Adopted park rules with catch-and-release policy
  • Approved Shaler-Riley Subdivision and related sewage planning
  • Adopted body camera and media policies, approved purchase of cameras
  • Accepted Horizon Construction property offer
  • Funded park maintenance purchases via recreation fund
  • Authorized drafting of a Police Chief job description for review at next meeting
  • Authorized personnel policy modernization using solicitor and union input
  • Emotional testimony by Supervisor Karen regarding the need for stronger police leadership, referencing a personal emergency response delay
  • Public comments praised the professionalism and civility of the meeting
  • Discussion confirmed that Robert's Rules of Order are not mandatory under Pennsylvania Sunshine Act, though decorum will be maintained
  • Next meeting scheduled for May 5, 2025
